## 1. Executive Summary

The centre enters 2026 from a position of relative strength: 2,847 licensed entities (+38 QoQ), $184.6B AUM hosted (+12.4% YoY), and a 31.2% market share across the GCC (+1.8 percentage points YoY). The Board's stated ambition is to grow AUM to $300B by 2030 while defending GCC market share above 30% in the face of intensifying competition from DIFC, ADGM, and GIFT City.

Three strategic priorities organise the 2026 work plan:

1. **Deepen the digital-assets and tokenisation franchise** — the centre will publish a refreshed Virtual Asset framework by Q3 2026 that lowers the minimum capital threshold for tokenisation issuers from $5M to $1.5M and extends the regulatory sandbox to Q4 2027. A dedicated Tokenisation Working Group will report to the CSO monthly.
2. **Win fund-domiciliation mandates out of Singapore and Hong Kong** — our sales team will run a targeted outreach programme to 12 mid-sized Asia-Pacific hedge funds (combined AUM ~$28B) that have publicly explored non-original-domicile options. Target: 4 conversions by EOY 2026.
3. **Build the green-finance and sustainable-investment proposition** — a new Green Bond issuance licence framework will go live in Q3 2026, with an allocation of $40M in co-marketing budget over 18 months.

## 2. Strategic Context

The competitive landscape has tightened materially since the 2024 strategy refresh. DIFC's recent Virtual Asset regime change has shifted at least 3 Singapore-based funds to public re-domiciliation discussions. GIFT City has signed bilateral MoUs with ADGM and Luxembourg on fund passporting. AIFC continues to capture Russia/CIS re-routing flows at scale.

Our internal pipeline data (Q1 2026) shows a 4% QoQ decline in fund-domiciliation deal volume, against a competitor-average increase of 12% QoQ. We are winning on regulatory sophistication but losing on speed-to-onboard and on tax-treaty coverage.

## 4. Key Risks

- **Digital-asset regulatory divergence** — our advantage on the Virtual Asset regime is time-limited. ESMA's MiCA RTS final standards and the EU AMLA beneficial-ownership standards will close the gap by mid-2027.
- **Talent attrition in senior relationship-manager band** — voluntary attrition is up 1.8pp YoY. Three of the last four senior departures cited DIFC partner-track opportunities.
- **Red Sea / Hormuz shipping disruption** — extended re-routing through Q4 2026 is compressing trade-finance margins by ~35bps across Gulf corridors.

## 5. Strategic Implications

The centre's competitive position is sound but the window for unilateral advantage is closing. The 2026 work plan is calibrated to convert today's regulatory and pipeline strengths into durable market-share gains before DIFC and GIFT City close the capability gap.

The CSO's strategic narrative for the year is: "Move first on tokenisation, win the Asia-Pac fund re-domicile wave, and make green finance a destination category — not an add-on."
