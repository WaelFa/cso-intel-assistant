## Agenda Item 1 — Opening & Quorum

The Chair confirmed quorum at 09:05. The agenda was approved without modification. The Chair noted that Director [redacted] had sent apologies and would be briefed separately by the CSO.

## Agenda Item 2 — Minutes of the Previous Meeting

The minutes of the Q4 2025 Board meeting were approved as a true and accurate record. Matters arising:

- **CAR-2025-22** (DIFC competitive benchmark) — the CSO confirmed closure. Action closed.
- **CAR-2025-24** (green-finance framework) — work in progress. Target completion revised to Q3 2026 (from Q2 2026). Reason: QFCA's green-bond framework publication shifted the comparator timeline.
- **CAR-2025-26** (Asia-Pac IR outreach) — work in progress. Action remains open.

## Agenda Item 3 — CSO Strategic Update

The CSO presented the Annual Strategy 2026 ("Bridge to 2030") document (attached as Annex A). Key points:

1. The centre enters 2026 from a position of relative strength: 2,847 licensed entities, $184.6B AUM, 31.2% GCC market share.
2. Three strategic priorities for the year: digital assets and tokenisation, fund-domiciliation mandates out of Singapore and HK, green finance as a destination category.
3. The CSO flagged three risks: digital-asset regulatory divergence (closing window), senior RM talent attrition (up 1.8pp YoY), Red Sea / Hormuz shipping disruption compressing trade-finance margins.

The Board discussed at length. Director [redacted] asked about the Q1 pipeline decline. The CSO confirmed the 4% QoQ fall in fund-domiciliation deal volume, attributed it to competitor moves (DIFC, GIFT City) and our slower speed-to-onboard, and proposed a time-to-onboard reduction programme to bring median onboarding from 47 days to 30 days by Q4 2026.

**Resolution 2026-Q1-01:** The Board approved the Annual Strategy 2026 as presented. The CSO is authorised to proceed with the three strategic priorities, the time-to-onboard reduction programme, and the proposed outreach to 12 Asia-Pac funds.

## Agenda Item 4 — Financial Performance

The CFO presented Q1 2026 financials. Headlines:

- Revenue: $284M (vs $267M Q1 2025, +6.4% YoY).
- Operating margin: 31.2% (vs 30.4% Q1 2025).
- Cash position: $1.8B (vs $1.6B at FY2025 close).
- AUM hosted: $184.6B (vs $164.2B Q1 2025, +12.4% YoY).

The CFO flagged that the trade-finance margin compression (-35bps across Gulf corridors due to Red Sea disruption) is expected to persist into H2 2026 and may cost $14-18M in revenue for the year. Mitigation: revised trade-finance pricing model (target launch Q3 2026) and selective exit from lowest-margin Gulf trade-finance clients.

**Resolution 2026-Q1-02:** The Board noted the Q1 2026 financial performance and approved the CFO's proposed trade-finance pricing model revision.

## Agenda Item 5 — Digital Assets Programme

The Head of Digital Assets (in attendance) presented an update on the Virtual Asset framework refresh:

- Drafting is complete. DFSA and the centre's legal team have aligned on a 60/40 risk-weighted capital treatment for tokenisation issuers below $1.5M threshold.
- Sandbox extension to Q4 2027 confirmed.
- Three Singapore-based funds (combined AUM ~$8.2B) are in active discussion for re-domiciliation once the new framework is published.
- Publication target: end of Q3 2026 (was previously Q4 2026).

The Board noted that the publication timeline is critical: DIFC's CP-2026-04 consultation closes in 8 weeks, and any slippage in our publication gives DIFC the unilateral advantage.

**Resolution 2026-Q1-03:** The Board approved accelerated publication of the refreshed Virtual Asset framework, with a hard target of end-Q3 2026. The Head of Digital Assets is the named owner.

## Agenda Item 6 — Internal Audit Annual Report

The Head of Internal Audit presented the FY2025 annual internal-audit report. Key findings:

- 4 medium-risk findings, 0 high-risk findings, 0 critical findings.
- All medium-risk findings relate to data-quality issues in the licensing pipeline (legacy systems, manual data entry).
- Remediation plan agreed with the COO, target completion Q4 2026.

**Resolution 2026-Q1-04:** The Board noted the FY2025 internal audit report and approved the remediation plan.

## Agenda Item 7 — Risk Report

The CRO presented the Q1 2026 risk dashboard (attached as Annex C). The Board noted:

- Overall risk posture: 🟡 Elevated.
- 🔴 Critical: digital-asset regulatory divergence (1 indicator).

**Resolution 2026-Q1-05:** The Board noted the Q1 2026 risk report and instructed the CRO to escalate any change in the digital-asset regulatory divergence indicator to the Board Chair within 48 hours.
